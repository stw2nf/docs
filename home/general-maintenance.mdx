<Danger>PAL is a piece of heavy duty, construction equipment. Improper assembly or disassembly can lead to unsafe behaviors and operational risks.</Danger>

# Guidelines
Always consult a Cyphra representative before attempting any servicing or maintenance beyond general cleaning.


## Power Off Procedures:
- **DO NOT** place hands or feet anywhere within PAL without first turning off the unit and disconnecting both batteries.
- **DO NOT** open the autopilot ("brain") without first turning off the unit and disconnecting both batteries.
- **DO NOT** perform any hardware maintenance (such as replacing sensors or tire) without first turning off the unit and disconnecting **both batteries**.

## Working with Motors and Sensors
To ensure safe operation, please adhere to the following guidelines when disconnecting or reconnecting motors and sensor connections:
<Steps>
  <Step title="Raise the PAL">
    Always elevate the PAL before working on motor and sensor connections. This provides access while ensuring stability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Function Testing">
    After any assembly or disassembly tasks, conduct a thorough functionality test before returning the robot to active use. This step is critical to verify that all systems are operating correctly and safely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Function Test Procedure
To ensure the safe and proper operation of the PAL following any assembly or disassembly, please follow the steps outlined below for the function test procedure.

**Test setup**
<Steps>
  <Step title="Raise the PAL">
    Elevate the robot off the ground, ensuring that it cannot move if the wheels spin at any speed. Ensure that the wheels are completely free of obstructions. Refer to the Lifting and Hoisting Procedures for Burro Maintenance for guidance on how to safely lift the unit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Power On the PAL">
    Once the robot is securely raised, power on the Burro Grande.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Function Testing Steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wait for UI to Load">
    Allow the user interface (UI) to fully load after powering on the device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Engage Manual Control">
    Stand to the side of the PAL for safety. Enter Manual Mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Directions">
    Confirm that commanding forward causes the robot to move forward. Confirm that commanding reverse causes the robot to move in reverse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Engage Person follow mode">
    Access the UI and activate a path. While the PAL is still elevated, observe the following: The robot should spin its wheels in the correct direction of travel. Observe the UI for robot sensing obstacles as you approach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final Steps">
    Once all checks are successfully performed, you may lower the PAL to the ground.The robot can now be put into service.
  </Step>
</Steps>
